## What Is Elements?

**Heading:** A community built on intimate scale, genuine bonds, and shared purpose.

**Body:**
Elements Workspace is a Montessori-inspired enrichment center in Gerritsen Beach, Brooklyn, built for homeschooling families with children ages 3–10. Our guides are experienced educators who understand child development in depth and who, over time, come to know each child in their group specifically — their interests, their pace, where they're ready to be stretched. From that knowledge, they prepare an environment where children have genuine choice and uninterrupted time to go deep.

**Three pillars:**

**Genuinely known.**
Guides accumulate real knowledge of each child — what they're drawn to, where they're ready for a challenge, what they need to settle and focus. That knowledge shapes the prepared environment every day.

**Developmentally prepared.**
Our approach is rooted in a method with one core principle: match the environment to what children at each developmental stage are genuinely ready for, then give them real choice within it and the uninterrupted time to use it. The result is a child who is appropriately challenged — and working in territory that genuinely engages them.

**Built on shared purpose.**
Every family at Elements has made a deliberate choice about education. That shared purpose shapes the culture of the space — how children relate to each other, how families connect. Small enough to know everyone. Intentional enough to build something together.

## Moonstones — Ages 6–9

**Tagline:** Where curiosity deepens into real work.

**Format:** Full-day drop-off · Core days Tue–Thu (3-day minimum) · Optional enrichment Mon and/or Fri

**Good fit if:**

## Our Philosophy

## Our Story

Elements Workspace was founded on a simple conviction: children learn best when the adults around them pay close attention to who they actually are.

We opened in 2023 in Gerritsen Beach — a tight-knit neighborhood at the edge of the city, with beaches, walking trails, and a pace that most people don't associate with New York. We built around homeschooling families who had made a deliberate choice about education and needed a partner who could deepen it.

In three years: a community of 30+, a 95% retention rate heading into year three. Families come for the program. They stay for what builds around it.

**Q: Is Elements a school?**
A: Elements is a homeschool enrichment center. Your child remains a homeschooler; you remain their primary educator. Depending on how you use it, Elements can anchor your child's week or complement a broader plan you've designed at home.

**Q: What does interest-based or child-led education mean?**
A: The environment your child works in is shaped by what they're developmentally ready for and what genuinely interests them. Our guides bring two kinds of knowledge: a thorough understanding of child development at this age band, and an accumulating knowledge of each specific child in the group. That combination informs how the environment is prepared and what gets offered each day.

**Q: What is the Montessori approach?**
A: Montessori is a complete educational method built around how children actually develop. Developmental readiness informs the prepared environment; the environment offers genuine choice; guides observe and respond rather than deliver a fixed curriculum; extended, uninterrupted work periods allow children to build real skill and self-direction. Montessori method shapes our thinking — on prepared environments, on developmental readiness, on what children need to do real work.

**Q: Do I need to be an experienced homeschooler to enroll?**
A: Families at Elements range from long-time homeschoolers to those just starting out. What matters is a genuine commitment to an educational path centered on your child's development — and a readiness to show up consistently. If you're new to homeschooling in New York, we're familiar with the regulations and happy to help you find your footing. If you're new to homeschooling in New York, we're familiar with the regulations and happy to help you find your footing.

**Q: Why is Moonstones a minimum of 3 days per week?**
A: The attunement between guides and children, the peer relationships, and the depth of work all require consistency to develop. We've learned, through experience, that three days is where consistency becomes community.

**Q: What does a typical day look like?**
A: In Moonstones, the day opens with community gathering, moves into extended independent work periods in reading, writing, and mathematics, includes outdoor time, and closes with a community activity. There's genuine structure — and within it, children have real choice about how they work and what they pursue.

In Starbirds, the morning follows a gentler rhythm: practical life work, pre-literacy and early numeracy through hands-on materials, creative and sensory activities, outdoor time, and snack.

**Q: How do I know if my child is ready for Moonstones?**
A: A visit is the best way to find out. Children who thrive in Moonstones can manage a full day away from home, sustain attention on a task independently, and are ready to be part of a community with real expectations. After your tour, your child will attend a full free trial day so we can assess mutual fit together.

**Q: Is Elements religious or secular?**
A: Secular. Non-religious curriculum and community. All backgrounds and beliefs welcome.

**Q: What's the enrollment process?**
A: It starts with a tour. Come see the space, meet the guides, ask your questions. If it feels like a fit, we'll walk you through enrollment from there.

**Q: Do you offer financial assistance?**
A: Yes, on a limited basis. Bring it up during your tour or reach out at jenny@elementsworkspace.com.

**Q: What ages do you serve?**
A: Starbirds: ages 3–6. Moonstones: ages 6–9. If your child is at an age boundary, a tour conversation will help find where they belong.

**Q: Where are you located? Is there parking?**
A: 2934 Gerritsen Ave, Gerritsen Beach, Brooklyn. Street parking available. Most accessible from Gerritsen Beach, Marine Park, Sheepshead Bay, Homecrest, Mill Basin, Bergen Beach and surrounding neighborhoods via the B31.

**Q: What if we need to withdraw mid-year?**
A: We ask for 45 days' written notice. This allows us to manage staffing and offer your spot to a family on our waitlist.

**Q: Are donations tax-deductible?**
A: Yes. Elements operates under the fiscal sponsorship of Metsada, a 501(c)(3) public charity. Donations are tax-deductible to the extent allowed by law.
